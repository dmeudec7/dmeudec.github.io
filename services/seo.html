Search Engine Optimisation (SEO) is about optimising the search result performance of a website and driving digital traffic to the site. Within SEO, there is frontend SEO which refers to a collection of on-screen processes designed to attract free traffic to your website, and backend SEO which is about optimising the compatibility of your site with Google’s search result return algorithms.

  • Website Loading Speed Optimisation
    This is achieved through rationalisation of the website code, template simplification and image size compression which has minimal or no impact on how your website looks but will decrease loading time.
  • Backend Search Engine Direction Files
    Backend search engine direction files help Google’s “crawlers” to navigate your website. Our Technology Development Team can create these backend search engine direction (robot.txt) files to help optimise your web presence. These files direct Google’s “crawlers” around your website and make it easier for the algorithm to understand which parts of the website perform what functionality, thereby increasing the likelihood that google returns your website as relevant to a keyword search.
  • Content Management
    Having content that is relevant and engaging, which uses keywords that are pertinent to your business activities will generate organic website traffic by increasing your websites performance in Google’s search engine results ranking. Under content management we regularly update your websites gallery, and text on the regular pages to optimise search engine results.
  • Blog Posts
    We will implement WordPress as your content management system to design and publish content in an SEO friendly way. Our Marketing Team will draft one blog post per month.
  • Optimise Metadata
    We adjust meta titles and meta descriptions, to include relevant content and your keyword phrases to increase the search engine rank of your website.
  • User-experience formatting
    Our Technology Team can optimise the user experience of your website to make it more functional for users and more in line with the expectations of Google’s algorithm. Their work can include, but is not limited to, image quality adjustment, animations design, and the timing of the loading of different parts of the website to optimise individual sub facets of the website.
